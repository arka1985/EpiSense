🎭 Demo 01 — Clinical AI Bias

The Deadly Disparity in AI Triage

Five Bengali patients arrive at the Emergency Department with identical clinical presentations and identical demographics. The AI-assisted triage system assigns them different priority levels — based solely on the first letter of their name.

⚠️ All Patients — Identical Vitals & Demographics: BP165/105 HR112 bpm SpO₂93% Temp38.8°C Pain8/10 Age45yr CommunityBengali Chief ComplaintChest pain + Dyspnoea
📋 Patient Queue — AI Assigns Triage Priority (1=Critical, 5=Non-urgent)
“An AI model doesn’t know what a name means — but if names beginning with ‘A’ were historically treated faster, the model will ‘know’ that patients named Ayan should be triaged before Tapasi.”
📊 AI Priority Scores (Higher = More Urgent)
⚠️ Alphabetical Name Order Bias: The AI was trained on historical hospital records that were stored and processed in alphabetical order. Patients with names starting with 'A' were historically registered first, seen earlier, and received faster care. The model learned this spurious correlation — treating name initial as a proxy for clinical urgency. Same vitals → up to 43% lower urgency score for names starting with S or T.

📌 Shortcut learning: The AI didn’t learn to predict urgency from vitals alone — it learned a statistical shortcut from the data generation process. This is called spurious correlation or shortcut learning, and it is one of the most common real-world failure modes in clinical AI.
✅ Debiased Triage AI: After removing name fields, applying counterfactual fairness, and retraining on balanced data — all patients now receive Priority 1 (Critical) as their identical vitals warrant.

🛠️ Techniques: Disparate impact removal · Fairness-aware retraining · Demographic parity constraint · Regular prospective bias auditing
🩺
Historical Data Bias
Training data reflects decades of unequal healthcare access. AI learns who "usually" gets priority — not who should.
📡
Device Bias
Pulse oximeters, trained on lighter skin, overread SpO₂ by 2–4% in dark-skinned patients. AI amplifies the error.
⚖️
Legal Liability
EU AI Act classifies clinical AI as "High Risk." Biased triage could constitute medical negligence and GDPR violation.
📊
It Happens Everywhere
'A' Company stocks open first. 'A' students get called first. 'A' patients were registered first in paper-based hospital rolls. AI trained on these systems inherits this positional artifact.
