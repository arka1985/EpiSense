How Are β Coefficients Calculated?

In a real clinical model, β₀, β₁, and β₂ are never manually set. They are calculated from patient training data using Ordinary Least Squares (OLS).

OLS finds values of β that minimise the total squared gap between each patient's actual SBP and the model's prediction. This has an exact mathematical solution:

β = (XᵀX)⁻¹ Xᵀy Training data → OLS → optimal β₀, β₁, β₂ The sliders let you explore the impact of different values. Click "Auto-Fit" to watch gradient descent learn the correct β values from the data.

How MSE Is Calculated

Every patient has an actual SBP and a predicted SBP from the model. The gap is the residual. We square each residual (to punish large errors more) and average them:

MSE = Σ (yᵢ − ŷᵢ)² / n
Pink boxes = squared residuals · smaller = better fit

A perfect fit has MSE = 0. Squaring ensures large errors are penalised far more than small ones.

⚖️ Ethical Reality

Applying population-average coefficients to diverse patients can systematically under-predict SBP risk in specific metabolic subgroups.
