K-Means Clustering Lab

K-Means groups patients into K clusters purely from their measurements — no diagnosis labels required. Watch centroids converge step-by-step.

The Algorithm

1. Place K centroids randomly 2. Assign each patient to nearest centroid: cluster(x) = argminₖ ‖x − μₖ‖² 3. Update: μₖ = mean(Cₖ) 4. Repeat 2-3 until convergence WCSS = Σₖ Σxᵢ∈Cₖ ‖xᵢ − μₖ‖² (lower = tighter clusters)

Elbow Method

WCSS always falls as K rises. Choose K at the elbow bend — where additional clusters give diminishing returns.

K too small → underfitting (groups mixed) K too large → overfitting (trivial clusters) K optimal → elbow in WCSS-vs-K curve

⚖️ Ethical Reality

K-Means uses Euclidean distance — so unscaled features (e.g., Glucose in mg/dL vs BMI) distort cluster shapes. Always standardise features before clustering to prevent spurious patient groupings.
