How Are β Coefficients Calculated?

In a real clinical model, β values are never manually chosen. They are learned from patient training data using Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE):

Training data → MLE → optimal β₀, β₁, β₂ MLE finds β values that make the observed outcomes (diabetic / not) most probable. Solved iteratively via gradient descent. The sliders let you explore — click "Auto-Fit" to learn betas from data.

Sensitivity & Specificity

When the model classifies each patient using the threshold, four groups emerge:

Sensitivity =TP/(TP+FN)
Of all diabetics, what % did we catch?
Specificity =TN/(TN+FP)
Of all healthy, what % were cleared?

Drag the yellow line — watch both metrics trade off live.

⚖️ Ethical Reality

A threshold of 0.5 is mathematically symmetric but clinically arbitrary. Missing one diabetic delays life-saving insulin. Thresholds must reflect clinical consequence — not mathematical convenience.
