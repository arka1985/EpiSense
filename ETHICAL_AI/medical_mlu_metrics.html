ROC & AUC Benchmarking

Imagine a new diagnostic test for a rare lung disease. How do we know if it's "good enough" for clinical use?

The **Receiver Operating Characteristic (ROC)** curve is the gold standard for measuring a model's ability to distinguish between healthy and sick patients.

The Overlap Problem

In the real world, "Sick" and "Healthy" markers often overlap. There is no perfect cut-off point.

If we set the threshold too low, we catch everyone (High Sensitivity) but flag many healthy people (False Positives). If we set it too high, we miss sick people (False Negatives).

The ROC Curve

By moving our threshold through every possible value, we trace a path: The **ROC Curve**.

We plot the **True Positive Rate** (Sensitivity) against the **False Positive Rate** (1 - Specificity). A perfect model hugs the top-left corner.

Area Under the Curve (AUC)

The **AUC** is a single number that summarizes the entire curve.
• **1.0**: Perfect Discrimination.
• **0.5**: No better than a coin flip.

AUC = Probability(Model ranks a random sick patient higher than a healthy one)

⚖️ Ethical AI Spotlight

The "Sensitivity Gap": A model can have a high overall AUC but still perform poorly for minority subgroups. Aggregated metrics like AUC can hide the fact that a test is significantly more accurate for one demographic than another.

Clinicians must audit "Disaggregated AUC" to ensure diagnostic parity across all patient populations.
