What is Qualitative Research?

Qualitative research is a fundamental scientific methodology emphasizing the collection and analysis of non-numerical data—such as text, video, or audio—to understand concepts, opinions, or human experiences. Rather than focusing on "how many", qualitative research seeks to answer the "how" and "why" behind phenomena. It relies heavily on textual coding and thematic induction.

Core Qualitative Methodologies

Phenomenology Explores and describes the "lived experience" of individuals regarding a specific phenomenon.
Grounded Theory Aims to inductively develop a unifying theory directly derived from the collected data.
Ethnography Involves immersing the researcher within the participants' natural social environment to observe culture.
Case Study An in-depth, multifaceted exploration of a specific entity, community, or bounded system in its real-life context.
Narrative Research Analyzes the personal stories people tell to understand how they construct meaning in their lives.
